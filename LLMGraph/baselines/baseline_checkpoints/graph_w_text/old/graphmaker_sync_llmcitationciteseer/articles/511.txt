The increasing adoption of voice-assisted systems in smart homes has raised concerns about their usability and accessibility for older adults. This study investigates the design of conversational interfaces that cater to the needs of this demographic. We conducted a series of user studies and expert evaluations to identify key challenges and opportunities for improvement. Our results highlight the importance of clear and concise voice prompts, personalized dialogue flows, and intuitive error handling mechanisms. We present a set of design guidelines and recommendations for creating inclusive conversational interfaces that support the needs of older adults, enabling them to fully benefit from voice-assisted technologies.
